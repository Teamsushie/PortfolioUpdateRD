The Eventfinder

This is the first group project I made during code bootcamp. The original idea was to create a trip planner that would connect real time to flight data and from there give you an option to book hotels, plan activities in the area, and book your flight - all in one. Unfortunately we quickly discovered that anything to do with Hospitality and Flight API's cost money that we weren't willing to shell out.

So what we created instead was this. Enter the "EventFinder". It hooks into the TicketMaster API based off the user search. It then shows 'events' in the area (using google maps API), complete with little pin drops on the exact venue. You can then buy tickets for the desired event.

Not exactly what we dreamed of, but hey, it works. Originally, I was in charge of the hotel API and used this awful program called "Amadeus" https://sandbox.amadeus.com/api-catalog after 10 hours of struggling my Instructor and TA's both looked at the documentation and immediately told me to find something else to use!

So I ended up floating around the group helping with whatever was needed. What I learned from this experience was a couple of things: 1.) Working on group projects is hard. Especially because of github, PR's are a pain in the butt, but when done right, they aren't scary at all. 2.) Coding is fun, once all the pieces start flowing together it's just one giany puzzle.
